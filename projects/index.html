Robotic Pose Estimation and Verification

February – September 2021 (8 months)

AIMS LabArtificially Intelligent Manufacturing Systems Lab | CDMECenter for Design and Manufacturing Excellence at Ohio State University

Project Details
  • Funded by the SecureAmerica Institute to detect cyberattcks on manufacturing systems, specifically robotics.
  • Aimed at estimating and verifing the position of a 6-axis robotic arm remotely.
  • Initial approach: detect specific points on the robot body, use trigonometry to calculate angles; DeepPoseRobot repo.
  • Final approach: use renderings of the robot in various positions paired with the detected robot body to estimate pose; RoPE-S3D repo.
  • I wrote and was responsible for all code in both of these repositories

Industry

A SharePoint-based Work Management System

  • I was tasked with recreating and improving a current work management system using Microsoft's Sharepoint as a platform.
  • I created a very flexible foundation of automation using Microsoft Power Automate, allowing for any aspect of the system (fields, relationships, etc.) to be changed via an Excel spreadsheet.
  • To facilitate user interaction, I created a GUI using Microsoft Power Apps that allowed for dynamic behavior depending on the current state of an item in the system.
  • The project was deemed as a successful proof-of-concept for further development and/or use of a Sharepoint-based system for work management.

Research

Autonomous Robotic Endotracheal Intubation

  • Endotracheal Intubation is the act of placing a tube through the throat and into the airway, allowing for a patient to be put on a ventilator.
  • I worked with another undergrad to train a YOLOv3 object-detection model to recognize landmarks in the human airway.
  • We used data collected from actual procedures as well as data collected by placing an endoscope in a mannequin.
  • This project ceased due to lack of funding

Research

The Effects of Pitch and Mode on the Perceived Emotional Valence of Musical Melodies

  • Although technically a 'class project', this was a project that I had to conceive and do on my own.
  • I was required to have an 'Expert Advisor' to consult throughout the project. I received help from Anna Kasdan, a PhD student at Vanderbilt in this role.
  • I collected data experimentally from ~275 subjects over the course of 3 days by playing clips of music in a random order.
  • The project culminated in a 5,000 word paper, available here, and a 15-minute presentation and oral defense (although I could have written and said so much more about the project).
